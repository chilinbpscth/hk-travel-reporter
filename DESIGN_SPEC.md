# Design assumptions

- Audience distance: one student using a school laptop at arm's length; the final poster is also shown to a small group.
- Narrative temperature: warm, curious and calm. The interface should feel like a field notebook, not a dashboard or a quiz.
- Visual reference: cream paper, mint green, dark green type and dotted paper texture from the supplied R'Odyssey reference; no copied logos.
- Hero detail: the A4 poster is the visual reward. It uses an AI illustration, accurate HTML text and a separately sourced map.
- Learning responsibility: the system supplies facts and sentence starters; the student selects, categorises, writes and orders the final report.
- Prototype scope: local-only student state, mock providers by default, no accounts or teacher dashboard.
